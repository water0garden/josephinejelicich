Nelson Carpentry School

In 2018 i did the 9 month full time furniture makers programme at the Centre for Fine Woodworking in Nelson. During the course we made a cabinet, a hall table with curves, a chair and then a final project (which i made a desk for ) all pieces were designed by me and made by me with the guidance of our teachers Brian Reed (cabinet), David Haig (Hall Table), Chair (Thorkild Hansen), Final project ( David Haig). The cabinet has a mix of half blind dovetail, stub tenon and mitred joinery, with frame and panel back. The Hall table curved elements are made with steam bent laminates, the parquet top is made with a mixture of the beeches (shop cut veneers), including some of the old deck from the nelson boat house which Thorkild was restoring, legs are hand shaped out of tawhai raunui ( hard beech). The Chair was made using American Cherry, laminated curved, back rests , hand cut mortise and tenon joinery, hand shaped rails and stiles. The desk was designed to be a small writing desk , with a top shelf for smaller items, each draw dovetailed by hand, the top has a groove for pencils.
